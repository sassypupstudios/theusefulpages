For informational purposes only — not medical advice. Ideal weight formulas were developed for clinical drug dosing, not fitness goals. A healthy weight depends on many individual factors including muscle mass, age, and bone density.

About the Formulas

Hamwi (1964) — Originally developed for insulin dosing. Males: 106 lbs for 5 ft + 6 lbs per inch over 5 ft. Females: 100 lbs + 5 lbs/inch.

Devine (1974) — Widely used in pharmacology and ICU settings. Males: 50 kg + 2.3 kg/inch over 5 ft. Females: 45.5 kg + 2.3 kg/inch.

Robinson (1983) — Derived from Metropolitan Life tables. Males: 52 kg + 1.9 kg/inch over 5 ft. Females: 49 kg + 1.7 kg/inch.

Miller (1983) — Another Met Life derivation. Males: 56.2 kg + 1.41 kg/inch over 5 ft. Females: 53.1 kg + 1.36 kg/inch.

How to use

  • Select your sex and preferred units (Imperial or Metric).
  • Enter your height.
  • Optionally enter your current weight to see your current BMI alongside the ideal range.
  • Click Calculate to see results from all four formulas.
  • The green healthy BMI range (18.5–24.9) is the most widely accepted guideline.
  • These formulas were designed for clinical use — use them as a general reference only.
