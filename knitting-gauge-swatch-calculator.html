How to Use This Calculator

Measuring Your Swatch

  • Knit a swatch at least 6"×6" using your project yarn and needles in the stated stitch pattern.
  • Wash and block the swatch before measuring — many yarns change size significantly after washing.
  • Place the swatch flat. Measure from the center, avoiding the edges (which are less accurate).
  • Count stitches and rows within a 4" area for the most accurate result.

Adjusting for a Pattern

  • Patterns state gauge as "X stitches over 4 inches" — enter that in the Pattern Gauge field.
  • Enter your swatch gauge in the Your Gauge field.
  • Enter either the pattern's cast-on count or your desired finished width.
  • The calculator will tell you how many stitches to cast on to achieve the correct size.

What the Percentage Difference Means

  • 0%: Your gauge matches exactly — use the pattern as written.
  • +5% or more: Your gauge is tighter — try one needle size larger.
  • -5% or more: Your gauge is looser — try one needle size smaller.
  • Differences over 10% may affect drape and fabric quality even after adjustment.

Results are estimates. Gauge, yarn, and needle type affect actual results. Always swatch before starting a garment.
