How to use

  • Enter a number in the top field and click Calculate to see its square root, cube root, and nth root.
  • Set the Nth root field to any value — for example, 4 gives you the 4th root (⁴√n).
  • Adjust Decimal places to control the precision of results (2–10 places).
  • Use the Power Calculator to compute n² (square), n³ (cube), or any base raised to an exponent.
  • The common square roots table below shows √1 through √20 for quick reference.
  • A Perfect Square badge tells you whether the number is a whole-number square.
