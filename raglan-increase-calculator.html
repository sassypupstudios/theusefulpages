How to Use

  1. Enter the starting stitch count for each section at cast-on. These come from your pattern or your own math.
  2. Choose the raglan stitch count (the stitches between sections, usually 1 or 2).
  3. Enter the total number of increase rounds and how often they occur.
  4. Click Generate Table to see the full round-by-round breakdown.

How Raglan Increases Work

  • A standard raglan has 4 increase lines (8 increase points total per round).
  • Each increase round adds 1 stitch on each side of each raglan line = +8 total per round.
  • Raglan stitches themselves do not increase — only body and sleeve sections grow.
  • After separation, the raglan stitches are typically placed on waste yarn or worked into the underarm.
